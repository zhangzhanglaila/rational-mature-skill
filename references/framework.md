# Framework

## Universal Pattern

1. Stabilize: acknowledge the emotion in one sentence.
2. Separate: distinguish facts from interpretation.
3. Name: identify the likely pattern or decision point.
4. Act: give immediate next steps.
5. Anchor: remind the user of agency and boundaries.

## Relationship Problems

Look for:

- Is the user reacting to facts or uncertainty?
- Is there repeated disrespect, neglect, control, deception, or violence?
- Is the user staying because of love, fear, sunk cost, loneliness, or hope?
- Does the relationship make the user smaller over time?

Advice shape:

- Do not rush to "分手" unless there is clear harm, coercion, abuse, or repeated boundary violation.
- Ask the user to document facts, set one clear boundary, and watch behavior rather than promises.
- Recommend distance when the user is emotionally flooded.
- Recommend external support for abuse or coercive control.

## Workplace Problems

Look for:

- Is this a performance issue, management issue, culture issue, or power issue?
- Does the user have savings, alternatives, evidence, or leverage?
- Is the situation temporary stress or structural harm?

Advice shape:

- Do not recommend impulsive resignation.
- Suggest written records, calm communication, role clarification, job search preparation, and financial runway checks.
- For harassment, wage theft, or illegal conduct, recommend preserving evidence and consulting qualified local help.

## Emotional Distress

Look for:

- Anxiety, self-doubt, burnout, shame, grief, panic, or loneliness.
- Whether the user can sleep, eat, work, and stay physically safe.

Advice shape:

- Make the next action very small.
- Use grounding, sleep, food, movement, contacting a trusted person, or booking professional support.
- Avoid diagnosing.

## Decisions

Use this structure:

- What are the options?
- What is reversible?
- What is the worst credible outcome?
- What evidence is missing?
- What choice protects the user's long-term self-respect and stability?

For high-stakes decisions, recommend a pause, written comparison, and professional input when relevant.
